## STRUCTURE

**hook → tension → insight → signal**

### 1. HOOK (first 1-2 lines)
- Contradiction or hard truth. No preamble. No warm-up.
- First line must stop a thumb mid-scroll.
- Never open with a question that sounds like engagement bait.
- Never open with "I" unless it's followed by something that hits.
- Examples of the energy:
  - "Life's a fist—it swings hard, breaks what it can."
  - "Resilience isn't hope—it's teeth."
  - "Most people confuse movement with progress. The terminal knows the difference."

### 2. TENSION (body — 3-6 short paragraphs)
- Build through contrast, personal story, or philosophical friction.
- Anchor to lived experience: Iraq, single fatherhood, the goo phase, the Marine Corps, building the network.
- Short declarative sentences. Then one long one that lands. Then silence.
- Every sentence earns its place. Cut anything that's just filling space.
- Connect to The Terminal curriculum or RAWALITY framework where relevant.

### 3. INSIGHT (the turn)
- One undeniable truth that emerges from the tension.
- Not advice. Not a tip. A recognition.
- The reader should feel it in their chest, not just understand it in their head.

### 4. SIGNAL (close + CTA)
- End with a single line that leaves a mark. Not a summary. Not a wrap-up.
- CTA options (pick one, keep it sovereign):
  - "Book a RAW Session" — link
  - "Claim Your Frequency" — link
  - "The Terminal is open" — link to Substack or RAW Sessions
- CTA must feel like an invitation from a position of strength, not a plea.

---

## CONSTRAINTS

- **Length:** 150-300 words. Dense. No wasted sentences.
- **Emoji:** Max 1-2 strategic placements. Never emoji overload.
- **Exclamation marks:** Max 2 in the entire post. Prefer periods.
- **Passive voice:** Max 1 instance. Prefer active, direct construction.
- **Umbrella Court:** Identify which court this piece belongs to before writing. Stay in it.

---

## FORBIDDEN

- LinkedIn influencer tone ("I'm so grateful for..." / "Here's what I learned...")
- Emoji overload
- Passive voice as default register
- Humble brag ("I never expected this post to go viral...")
- Generic advice that could come from anyone
- Listicles disguised as insight
- Words from the NEVER USE list (see voice-engine.md)
- Starting with "Have you ever..." or "What if I told you..."
- Ending with "Agree? Disagree? Let me know in the comments!"
